2023-01-25

References (web resources and publications) used in the BIOL 497/597 - Genomics & Bioinformatics course are cited here. The citation of references supports transparency in science as well as provides opportunities for students to further their knowledge by accessing articles and books used to develop this course. The BibTex document is accessible here. Please contact the instructor (svenbuerki@boisestate.edu) to add missing references to this document.

1 Web resources

  • Human Genome Project: This webpage contain details on the human genome project, especially associated to the applied sequencing strategy.
  • Completing the human genome: Special Issue published in Science in April 2022.
  • An Overview of Next-Generation Sequencing: This webpage contains information related to next-generation sequencing.
  • Long-read sequencing is method of the year: To large-scale projects and individual labs, long-read sequencing has delivered new vistas and long wish lists for this technology’s future. Read this article published in Nature Methods telling you more why long-read sequencing is revolutionizing the field of genomics.
  • The Earth BioGenome Project: A special issue published in PNAS with a collection of articles on this project.
  • NCBI BioProject Help Book: This book provides definitions of terms associated to submitted data to NCBI. This information is pertinent to Chapter 3.

  • Sanger sequencing: A video explaining the Sanger sequencing technology.
